About Me

I am currently an Assistant Professor in the Department of Communications and Networking at XJTLU. I received my B.Sc. in Telecommunications Engineering from KNUST (2014), and my M.Eng. (2019) and Ph.D. (2023) in Computer Science & Technology from UESTC. I later served as a Postdoctoral Researcher with the Hybrid Positioning Research Group (UESTC) and a Postdoctoral Fellow at the KU 6G Research Center, Khalifa University.

I design contract-theoretic and reinforcement learning frameworks for shared Automated Valet Parking & Charging (AVPC) and intelligent transportation systems, and explore LLM-assisted scheduling for network/service management. My interests include 5G/6G networks, vehicular networks, target positioning, blockchain, game theory, and multi-agent RL.

Prospective Students: I’m seeking self-motivated students for LLM-aided optimization in Autonomous Driving (AD) and AVP. Email: Gordon.Boateng@xjtlu.edu.cn
